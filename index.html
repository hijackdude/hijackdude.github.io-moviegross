THE TIME OF ACTION MOVIES

The mutiple line charts below show the change in gross of different kinds of movies between 1986 and 2016. You can slide your mouse to explore the trends.

Comedy is standing still

In the past,Action and Comedy were long the highest-grossing movies combined. In 1986, the comedy genre ranked first among all film genres with a total box office of $1.11 billion, Thirty years later, in 2016, action movies grossed $4.65 billion at the box office, while comedies grossed just $1.09 billion. Action movies have made four times as much money as comedies.

What has caused the overall box office of comedy to stagnate for 30 years? Is there a huge problem with the development of comedy ? Don't. In fact, as early as 1986, the number of comedic (mainly Hollywood) genre films worldwide reached the limited edition of its box office. The top grossing comedy film of 1986 was Back to School , which earned $91.25 million. As a low-cost family comedy, it also ranked third in the box office in 2016. In 2016, the worldwide smash hit “la la land” took in just $150 million.

Action movie makes a difference

On the other hand, the number of action movie gross increased from $800 million to $4 billion throughout the period. Two factors attribute to the continuous rise: the deep operation of IP movies and the strong use of visual technology. In 1989, action films like Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade could bring companies about $190 million and were the top three grossing films in North America, while the classic comedy Home Alone grossed $280 million in 1990. At that time, Indiana Jones was a popular style of action films.

However, in the 21st century, action movies gradually develop towards the direction of big IP, big production and big special effects. Taking the Star Wars movies as an example, on the one hand, after mature commercial operation and packaging, the classic elements such as lightsaber duel and darth vader in the movies have strong recognition in the hearts of audiences, especially those born in the 1980s and 1990s, and become the place of their faith. Once that audience has become a major consumer of the market, once there's a lightsaber fight on screen, they're bound to make a pilgrimage to the theater, not to a romantic comedy. On the other hand, there are fewer scenes and more realistic effects. Big IP+ big special effects action movies, and other movies increasingly seem not to be in the same dimension.

Cartoons have risen to prominence

Statistics show that the box office performance of animated films has experienced a miraculous rise in the past 30 years. From about $100 million in 1986 to nearly $2.5 billion in 2016. This is also due to the victory of big IP+ big effects. In 1996, pixar made toy story for Disney, which became a milestone for animated films. Animated films have produced one classic after another for us: shrek, nemo, cars, baymax... On the other hand, advances in 3D technology have also made animated films less youthful and cooler.

VOTES AND BOX OFFICE

Are the imdb votes really to the movie gross?

The final shape of the scatter plot is very interesting: almost all the points are in the lower right of the axis, and almost none are in the upper left. Looking at this diagram for the first time, I'm sure you'll agree with us that they are related. The higher the score, the higher the box office, or the higher the box office, the higher the score.

However, we analyzed the correlation between the two variables. In fact, the correlation coefficient of the two variables was only 0.16, almost no correlation. That makes sense in real life: highly rated art often underperforms at the box office. The shawshank redemption was a case in point. The film grossed just over $20 million when it was released in 1994, costing its distributor $30 million.

Many of the highest-grossing movies are at a pass-level of 5-7, and when you reach that level, you're a movie of quality. But we also notice that there are almost no points at the top left of the table, and there's a magic line that comes out of the table, and there are almost no points at the top left of the table. At the same time, if you look closely at the scatter plot, you will find that the highest box office of the movie with the same score increases as the score gradually increases.

This reminds us that word of mouth may not necessarily have a significant positive correlation with box office: that is, if word of mouth is good and everyone goes to see your movie, but word of mouth has a pass mark, and when your score is low, it will definitely affect the box office. A high score doesn't necessarily mean a high box office. A low score doesn't necessarily mean a high box office. That's a bit harsh for our filmmakers, isn't it
