Action movies makes a difference

It can be seen that the number of action movie gross increased from $800 million to $4 billion throughout the period. Two factors attribute to the continuous rise: the deep operation of IP movies and the strong use of visual technology. In 1989, action films like Indiana Jonesand The Last Crusade could bring companies about $190 million and were the top three grossing films in North America, while the classic comedy Home Alone grossed $280 million in 1990. At that time, Indiana Joneswas a popular and traditional style of action films.

However, action movies gradually develop towards big IP,big production and special effects in the last decade. For instance, Star Wars , on the one hand, is packaged by mature commericial work. This caused a series of stuff that can play an important role in pupil's life.The audience especially born in 1980s and 90s have strong recognition for the classic elements such as lightsaber duel and darth vader in the movies. Once that audience has become a major consumer of the market,they're bound to make a pilgrimage to the theater to see Star Wars rather than a comedy, On the other hand, there are fewer scenes and more realistic effects. Big IP effects action movies, and other movies increasingly seem not to be in the same dimension.

Cartoons have risen to prominence

Data shows that the box office of animated films has experienced a miraculous rise in the past 30 years. From about $100 million in 1986 to nearly $2.5 billion in 2016. This is also due to the victory of big IP+ big effects. In 1996, Pixar made toy story for Disney, which became a milestone as a ranimated film. Animated films have produced one classic after another for us like Shrek, Nemo.

Comedy stands still

The gross of comedies fluctuated between 1986 and 2016 when the figure for action movies rose significantly in the period. It is noticeable that number of comedies had decreased from 2011 to 2016. What factors have caused the overall comedy box office to stagnate in the period? Is there a huge problem with the development of comedies?

To be more precisely, the comedic genre films have made full use of its gross potential when other genres like action and animation are able to dig out more. For example, the top grossing comedy film in 1986 was Back to Schoolwhich earned $91.25 million. As a low-cost comedy, it ranked third in the box office in 2016. However, the worldwide smash hit La La land grossed just $150 million in 2016.

No relations between votes and movie gross

As you can see, the circles accmulates intensively in the right part of the chart. You can click diffrent buttons to check.It seems that there are some relations between the two variables. However, we analyzed the correlation between the two variables by math tools. In fact, the correlation coefficient of the two variables was only 0.16. It makes sense in reality. Highly rated movies often underperforms at the box office.

Many of the highest-grossing movies are at a vote of 5-7. It is noticeable that there are almost no points at the top left of the table, and there's a magic line that comes out of the table, At the same time, if you look closely at the scatter plot, you will find that the highest box office of the movie with the same score increases as the score gradually increases.

It reminds us that vote may not necessarily have a significantly positive correlation on box office. A high score doesn't necessarily mean a high box office. A low score doesn't necessarily mean a high box office. That's a bit harsh for our filmmakers, isn't it?
